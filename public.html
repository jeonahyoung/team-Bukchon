The Baeryeom House

Finding directions

09:00 ~ 18:00 (Last Visit: 17:30)

The Baeryeom House was the residence of the painter Baeryeom (Je-dang) Bae Ryeom (1911–1968) from 1959 until his death in 1968. Baeryeom was a student of Cheongjeon Lee Sang-beom and is regarded as a painter who continued the tradition of Korean ink landscape painting.Before Baeryeom lived there, the house was occupied by Song Seok-ha (1904–1948), a historically significant figure.

Hong Geon-ik House

Finding directions

09:00 ~ 18:00 (Last Visit: 17:30)

The Hong Geon-ik House in Pilun-dong is a traditional Korean hanok built between 1934 and 1936. Situated on a 740.5 square meter plot, the house consists of five buildings—the main gate quarters, servants' quarters, men's quarters, women's quarters, and a detached building— all naturally arranged along a gentle slope. It is the only remaining hanok in Seoul that features an il-gakmun (a traditional single-arched gate), a well, and an ice storage room (binggo).

Bukchon Lounge

Finding directions

10:00 ~ 21:00 (Last Visit: 20:00)

Bukchon Lounge, one of Seoul’s public hanoks, is an open space for all visitors to Seoul and Bukchon. It offers a rich experience of the charm of traditional hanok architecture and a variety of cultural activities.From the main quarters where you can brew traditional tea with a tea sommelier, to the men's quarters offering views of the inner courtyard and yard, and the detached building showcasing the beauty of traditional hanok roof tiles—Bukchon Lounge invites you to stay, enjoy, and relax.

Bukchon Hanok Heritage Center

Finding directions

10:00 ~ 18:00 (Last Visit: 17:00)

The Bukchon Hanok Heritage Center is a space that introduces modern urban-style hanoks, which served as a cultural bulwark against Japanese encroachment into Bukchon during the Japanese colonial period. These urban-style hanoks are small-scale traditional houses built by dividing larger plots of land, forming the foundation for Koreans to settle in the Bukchon area.

Bukchon Traditional Craft Experience Center

Finding directions

10:00 ~ 17:00 (Last Visit: 16:30)

The Bukchon Traditional Craft Experience Center is a place where visitors can discover and experience Korea’s outstanding traditional crafts. Three different craft programs are offered each day of the week, allowing participants to choose and enjoy a craft experience that suits their interests. Local artisans active in Bukchon lead the programs themselves, sharing the beauty of traditional Korean crafts with visitors.

Bukchon Cultural Center

Finding directions

09:00 ~ 18:00 (Last Visit: 17:30)

The Bukchon Cultural Center is a central gathering place that promotes Seoul’s hanoks and is recognized as the first official public hanok desig nated by the city of Seoul.The center is a restored hanok that was once the home of Min Hyeong-gi, a high-ranking official in the Ministry of Finance during the late Joseon Dynasty. Built before 1900, it is a quintessential example of a nobleman’s house in Bukchon. In 2002, the Seoul Metropolitan Government acquired and renovated the property as part of the Bukchon Preservation Project and opened it as the Bukchon Cultural Center.

Bikdam: Traditional Fermentation Workshop

Finding directions

11:00 ~ 17:00 (Last Visit: 16:30)

This is a space where visitors can learn about and experience the healthy foods and beverages of Korea’s ancestors through various approaches to traditional fermentation. Depending on the season, participants can try making a variety of traditional Korean foods and drinks. You can even make your own fermentation starters, such as nuruk (traditional fermentation agents like ihwagok or wheat nuruk).

Dongnim Maedeup Workshop

Finding directions

10:30 ~ 17:30 (Last Visit: 17:00)

The Dongnim Maedeup Workshop is housed within a traditional Korean hanok, where its exhibition space beautifully blends historical artifacts, faithful reproductions, and modern creations—highlighting the timeless beauty of Korean decorative knotting, known as maedeup.The exhibit features a comprehensive collection of traditional ornamental knots such as norigae (pendants), belts, pouches, thread winders, and tassels (yuso), as well as materials used in knot-making, including threads, cords, and accessories.

Bukchon Bingwan

Finding directions

10:30 ~ 17:30 (Last Visit: 17:00)

Bukchon Bingwan is a high-end hanok accommodation facility located in the scenic Gahoe-dong area of Jongno-gu. It is the first example of a Seoul public hanok that combines profitability with public accessibility.The above-ground floors offer traditional hanok lodging spaces, while the basement houses a community lounge called The Living Room, designed for local residents to relax comfortably.

Bukchon HanokCheong

Finding directions

10:00 ~ 17:30 (Last Visit: 17:00)

Bukchon Hanokcheong is a public hanok located on a hillside at 11 Gahoe-dong, notable for its relatively large size of about 120 pyeong (approximately 397 square meters), which is rare in Bukchon. Bukchon Hanokcheong operates as a shared hanok space where citizens create and enjoy hanok-related cultural content together. It is open primarily for free public exhibitions by citizens and supports a variety of cultural and educational activities such as lectures, performances, and forums linked to these exhibitions.

Traditional Hongyeom Workshop

Finding directions

10:00 ~ 18:00 (Last Visit: 17:00)

Traditional Hongyeom Workshop is the studio of Master Kim Kyung-yeol, holder of Seoul City Intangible Cultural Property No. 49 in Hongyeom dyeing. Here, visitors can admire a variety of restored works reproduced using the traditional hongyeom dyeing technique.Hongyeom is a dyeing method using safflower (honghwa), which was one of the main crafts practiced by ten master artisans each in the Joseon Dynasty’s Sanguiwon and Jeyonggam, royal government offices responsible for court attire.

Saeksil Munyang Nubi Workshop

Finding directions

10:00 ~ 17:00 (Last Visit: 16:00)

Saeksil Munyang Nubi Workshop is a studio where traditional nubi quilting artworks, made by sewing strips of hanji paper between layers of fabric, are exhibited and where visitors can directly experience the craft. The workshop is run by Master Kim Yoon-sun, who was inspired to pursue saeksil nubi after discovering a quilted pouch (nubi ssamji) handmade by her great-grandmother while organizing her grandfather’s belongings.
